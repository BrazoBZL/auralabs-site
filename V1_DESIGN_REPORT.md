**Aura Labs | Research-Grade Peptides**
**June 8, 2026**

---

## Design Philosophy

**Center of Gravity: Intellectual Honesty**

Every element either reinforces honesty or compromises it. The homepage communicates discipline through:
- **Structural restraint** (generous white space, minimal decoration)
- **Evidence over narrative** (specifications over storytelling)
- **Respect for researcher competence** (no hand-holding, assume expertise)
- **Transparency about limitations** (what we don't know matters as much as what we do)

## Design Decisions Explained

### WHAT WAS REMOVED
| Element | Why | Impact |
|---------|-----|--------|
| "Premium" language | Marketing, not honesty | Cleaner positioning |
| "99%+ Purity Guaranteed" | Claim without context | Replaced with actual specs |
| Trust bar with promises | Performance, not evidence | Replaced with documented specs |
| About section | Narrative, not data | Replaced with "Why This Section" |
| Email popup | Assumes need for incentive | Removed (assumes self-selection) |
| Age gate on homepage | Friction before value | Kept for compliance, not here |
| Product categories | Marketing organization | Replaced with pure specs |
| Navigation menu | Distraction | Removed for v1 focus test |

### WHAT WAS ADDED
| Element | Why | Impact |
|---------|-----|--------|
| "Limitations" section | Transparency about uncertainty | Signals honesty |
| "How We Maintain Standards" | Shows process, not just results | Builds confidence in rigor |
| "What Research-Grade Means" | Defines without marketing | Researcher can judge fit |
| Exact specs (MW, structure, format) | Verification, not claims | Scientist reads it as credentials |
| Known limitations (italic, separate) | Signals awareness of unknowns | Separates evidence from hope |
| 4-step process (Select→Request→Verify→Proceed) | Clarity + no hand-holding | Respects researcher autonomy |

## Design Principles Tested

**Does this show discipline or performance?**
✅ **DISCIPLINE** — Sparse layout, no decoration, every element functional

**Does it present evidence or tell a story?**
✅ **EVIDENCE** — Specifications over narrative, data over claims

**Does it trust the researcher or coddle them?**
✅ **TRUST** — No explanations, no reassurance, assumes competence

**Does every element communicate honesty?**
✅ **YES** — Each section explains methodology, shows specs, admits unknowns

## What This Version Tests

1. **Does minimal hero work for research compounds?**
   - Hypothesis: "We offer what we're certain of" lands better than marketing positioning
   - Test: Researcher engagement on landing page

2. **Do product specs + limitations build more trust than promises?**
   - Hypothesis: Transparency about unknowns signals honesty
   - Test: Conversion rate from researchers (not general audience)

3. **Does explaining the process increase confidence?**
   - Hypothesis: Showing HOW you maintain standards matters more than saying you do
   - Test: Time on "Why This Section" + click-through to compounds

   - Hypothesis: No marketing = "these people know what they're doing"
   - Test: Sentiment from researcher interviews

## Summary

Version 1 is built entirely around **intellectual honesty as the center of gravity.**

It communicates through:
- **Structural restraint** (no decoration, generous space)
- **Transparent specs** (structure, MW, purity, format)
- **Honest limitations** (what we don't know)
- **Process clarity** (how we work, what happens next)
- **Respect for expertise** (no hand-holding, assume competence)

The homepage doesn't try to convince. It shows what you're getting and trusts you to decide if it fits your research.

That's the bet.
