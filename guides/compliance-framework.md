# Research Peptide Compliance Framework

## Introduction

Regulatory compliance for peptide research is complex and varies by jurisdiction, institution, and research type. This guide provides researchers with frameworks to understand FDA, WADA, institutional, and international standards.

**Not legal advice** — consult institutional legal and compliance teams for your specific circumstances.

---

## 1. FDA Classification of Peptides

### Research vs. Clinical Use

#### Research-Grade Peptides (IND Exempt)

**Definition:** Peptides used in non-human research that are NOT intended for clinical use.

**Key characteristics:**
- Used in vitro (cell cultures) or animal models
- Not administered to humans
- Not intended for therapeutic development
- Subject to fewer regulations

**FDA Requirements:**
- Manufacturer must ensure purity & quality
- Documentation (CoA) should be maintained
- No specific FDA pre-approval needed for research use
- But: Institutional biosafety committees (IBC) may have requirements

**Compliance checklist:**
- [ ] Supplier provides CoA with purity data
- [ ] HPLC or MS confirms identity
- [ ] Batch traceability documented
- [ ] Storage & handling conditions documented
- [ ] IBC approval obtained (if animal studies)

#### Clinical-Grade Peptides (IND Required)

**Definition:** Peptides intended for human testing or therapeutic development.

**FDA Requirements:**
- **Investigational New Drug (IND) Application** required before human testing
- Comprehensive safety & efficacy data
- Manufacturing & quality standards (GMP: Good Manufacturing Practice)
- Pharmacokinetics & toxicology studies (pre-clinical)
- Protocol approval (Institutional Review Board - IRB)

**Scope:** Only clinical trials or FDA-authorized research use.

**For researchers:** Most university research uses research-grade peptides (IND exempt).

## 2. Research Peptide Regulations by Institution

### Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC)

**What is IBC?**
Federal requirement for institutions receiving NIH funding or conducting recombinant DNA research.

**IBC reviews:**
- Peptide source & purity
- Proposed use (in vitro, animal, human)
- Potential hazards
- Containment procedures

**Approval process:**
1. Researcher submits protocol to IBC
2. IBC reviews risk assessment
3. IBC votes approval/conditional approval/denial
4. Researcher must follow approved protocol

**Documentation to provide:**
- Protocol summary (objectives, methods, peptide details)
- Supplier information (Aura Labs, batch #, purity)
- Safety assessment (known hazards of specific peptide)
- Containment plan (how will contamination/exposure be prevented)
- Training records (researcher biosafety training)

### Institutional Review Board (IRB)

**When required:** If research involves human subjects.

**Documentation:**
- Informed consent forms (ICF)
- Risk assessment
- Data protection plan
- Adverse event monitoring

**For most university research:** If using research-grade peptides in vitro or animal models only, IRB approval is NOT required (IBC only).

**If human subjects:** Both IBC and IRB approval required.

## 3. FDA Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)

### What is GMP?

A set of guidelines ensuring pharmaceutical products are consistently produced and controlled to quality standards.

**GMP covers:**
- Manufacturing facilities (cleanrooms, equipment validation)
- Personnel qualifications
- Raw material sourcing & testing
- Manufacturing processes & controls
- Quality assurance & testing
- Documentation & traceability
- Stability testing

### Do Research-Grade Peptides Need GMP?

**Short answer:** Not legally required by FDA for research use.

**But:** Quality is critical.

**Aura Labs approach:**
- Manufacturing partner ISO 17025 accredited
- GLP testing (Good Laboratory Practice)
- Stability tested
- Batch documentation complete
- NOT marketed as GMP-grade, but meets GMP-equivalent standards

### What Researchers Should Verify

| Criterion | Check With Supplier |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Lab Accreditation** | ISO 17025, GLP certification |
| **Purity Testing** | HPLC data, MS confirmation |
| **Batch Stability** | Stability data available |
| **Storage Conditions** | Documented -20°C or -80°C |
| **Batch Traceability** | Unique batch number |
| **CoA Completeness** | HPLC, MS, water content, identity |

## 4. WADA (World Anti-Doping Agency) Classification

### Why WADA Matters for Research

If your research is NCAA, international sports, or Olympic-related, WADA compliance is critical.

### WADA Prohibited List

**Growth hormone and secretagogues:**
- Growth hormone (GH) — PROHIBITED (anabolic)
- Ghrelin receptor agonists — PROHIBITED (ipamorelin, GHRP analogs)
- GHRH analogs — PROHIBITED (CJC-1295)

**Peptides NOT on prohibited list (research-applicable):**
- BPC-157 — NOT on WADA list (allowed for research)
- TB-500 — NOT on WADA list (allowed for research)
- Semaglutide — NOT on WADA list (GLP-1 agonist, not GH-related)

### Compliance for Athletic Research

**If studying athlete subjects:**
- Research involving GH secretagogues must disclose WADA status
- Athletes cannot legally use prohibited compounds
- Informed consent must include WADA prohibition notice
- Protocol must restrict to non-athlete populations or pre-authorized research

**Monitoring:** WADA updates prohibited list annually (September 1). Verify current status before research initiation.

## 5. International Regulations

### Canada (HPFB - Health Products Regulatory Authority)

**Research peptides:** Not subject to NHP (Natural & Non-prescription Health Products) or DIN licensing.

**Requirements:**
- Supplier documentation
- Purity verification
- Storage documentation
- Institutional approval (IBC if relevant)

**No special compliance beyond U.S. standards.**

### European Union (EMA - European Medicines Agency)

**Classification:** Research-grade peptides fall under "research reagents."

**Requirements:**
- EU institution must have CE mark (quality certification)
- Supplier compliance with EU GMP Annex 15 (for research)
- REACH compliance (chemical safety)
- Documentation & traceability

**Import to EU:** Peptide source must meet EU standards. Verify supplier compliance.

### Australia (TGA - Therapeutic Goods Administration)

**Research peptides:** Not considered therapeutic goods if for research only.

**Requirements:**
- Institutional approval (similar to U.S. IBC)
- Supplier documentation
- Purity & identity verification
- No TGA pre-approval needed for research

### China, Japan, South Korea

**Regulations vary significantly.** Researchers must consult local institutional guidelines and regulatory authorities before import.

## 6. Institutional Documentation Checklist

### Before Starting Research

**Supplier documentation:**
- [ ] Certificate of Analysis (CoA) for exact batch
- [ ] HPLC purity (≥95%)
- [ ] Molecular weight confirmation (MS or calculated)
- [ ] Water content (≤5% for lyophilized)
- [ ] Lab accreditation (ISO 17025)
- [ ] Batch number & synthesis date
- [ ] Storage conditions & shelf life

**Institutional approval:**
- [ ] IBC protocol submitted & approved
- [ ] IRB approval (if human subjects)
- [ ] Biosafety training completed & documented
- [ ] Principal Investigator (PI) signature
- [ ] Safety officer approval (if required by institution)

**Research protocol:**
- [ ] Written protocol (objectives, methods, peptide details)
- [ ] Risk assessment (hazard analysis)
- [ ] Containment procedures
- [ ] Adverse event monitoring plan
- [ ] Data protection plan (if human subjects)

### During Research

**Ongoing documentation:**
- [ ] Batch-specific records (receipt date, CoA reference)
- [ ] Storage conditions verified (temperature monitoring logs)
- [ ] Personnel & training records
- [ ] Adverse events (if any) documented
- [ ] Deviations from protocol logged

### After Research

**Final documentation:**
- [ ] Results & publications
- [ ] Unused peptide disposal (compliance with institutional waste protocols)
- [ ] Final report to IBC (if required)
- [ ] Data retention (typically 5-7 years)

## 7. Special Considerations by Peptide Type

### Growth Hormone Secretagogues (Ipamorelin, CJC-1295, etc.)

**Additional compliance:**
- WADA prohibited (if athletic research, disclose in protocol)
- IGF-1 monitoring may be required (institutional policy)
- Hormonal effects documentation required
- Informed consent emphasizes endocrine effects (if human subjects)

**Approval timeline:** 2-4 weeks (IBC review, often flagged for hormone use)

### Tissue Repair Peptides (BPC-157, TB-500)

**Additional compliance:**
- NOT on WADA prohibited list (athlete-friendly)
- Minimal systemic effects (lower safety concern)
- Standard IBC approval usual

**Approval timeline:** 1-2 weeks (lower scrutiny)

### Weight Loss / Metabolic Peptides (Semaglutide, Tirzepatide analogs)

**Additional compliance:**
- GLP-1 agonists NOT on WADA list
- Metabolic effects documentation
- Blood glucose monitoring (if diabetic subjects)
- Nausea/GI side effect monitoring

**Approval timeline:** 2-3 weeks

### Custom/Novel Peptides

**Additional compliance:**
- Toxicology data may be required
- Literature review on similar compounds
- More rigorous safety assessment
- Longer IBC review (3-6 weeks)

## 9. Publication Compliance

### What to Disclose in Methods Section

**Peptide sourcing:**
> [Compound] was obtained from Aura Labs (Batch #[XXXXX], [X%] purity by HPLC, 
> synthesized [Month/Year]). Certificate of Analysis is available upon request.

**Institutional approval:**
> This study was approved by the [Institution] Institutional Biosafety Committee 
> (Protocol #[XXXXX]) and [IRB if applicable] (Protocol #[XXXXX]).

**Regulatory compliance:**
> All animal procedures were conducted in accordance with the Guide for the Care and 
> Use of Laboratory Animals (8th edition). Human subjects research was conducted in 
> compliance with the Declaration of Helsinki and institutional IRB guidelines.

**WADA (if applicable):**
> Note: [Compound] is on the WADA Prohibited Substance List. This research was 
> conducted with non-athlete subjects and is for research purposes only.

### Example Full Disclosure

```
MATERIALS & METHODS

Peptide Administration:
Ipamorelin (Aura Labs, Batch #AR-IPA-20260520, 98% purity by HPLC, 
synthesized May 2026) was reconstituted in sterile 0.9% saline and 
administered at 200 mcg per dose via subcutaneous injection three times 
daily for 12 weeks. Certificate of Analysis is available upon request 
from the corresponding author.

Ethical Approval:
This study was approved by the University of [X] Institutional Biosafety 
Committee (Protocol #2026-IBC-456) and Institutional Review Board 
(Protocol #2026-IRB-789). All subjects provided informed consent prior 
to enrollment. The study was conducted in accordance with the Declaration 
of Helsinki and institutional guidelines.

Regulatory Compliance:
Ipamorelin is listed on the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) Prohibited 
Substance List (Anabolic Agents category). This research was conducted 
with non-athlete adult subjects (aged 30-50) for the purpose of 
investigating growth hormone secretion and metabolic effects.
```

## 10. Red Flags: Non-Compliant Suppliers

| Red Flag | Issue |
|----------|-------|
| No CoA provided | Cannot verify purity or identity |
| No batch number | Cannot trace source or reproduce |
| No lab accreditation listed | Testing not verified by third party |
| Vague purity ("approximately 95%") | No specific data |
| "Not for human consumption" only disclaimer | Insufficient for research compliance |
| No storage conditions specified | Peptide may be degraded |
| Refuse institutional inquiries | Avoid these suppliers |
| No batch stability data | Unknown shelf life & degradation |

## 11. Troubleshooting: Compliance Questions

### "Can I use supplier X's peptide without IBC approval?"

**No.** If your institution receives federal funding (NIH, NSF) or uses recombinant DNA, IBC approval is required. Even without funding, most institutions require approval for safety.

### "My institution doesn't have an IBC. What do I do?"

Contact your **Institutional Biosafety Officer** or **Environmental Health & Safety (EHS) department.** They'll direct you to appropriate approval pathway.

### "Can I order peptides to my home for personal research?"

**No.** Research must be conducted in accredited institutional facilities. Unaccredited home research is not compliant.

### "Is research-grade peptide legal for me to own?"

**In the U.S.:** Yes, research-grade peptides are legal to purchase, own, and use for research. But use must be in approved institutional settings.

### "What if I publish without institutional approval?"

**Consequences:** Retraction of publication, damage to PI reputation, possible loss of funding eligibility. Institutional approval is non-negotiable.

## 12. Compliance Timeline: Sample 12-Week Protocol

```
Week 1:
- Day 1: Submit IBC protocol
- Day 3: IBC preliminarily reviews
- Day 5: IBC meeting (approval or conditional approval)

Week 2:
- Day 1: Address IBC feedback (if any)
- Day 3: Final IBC approval received

Week 3:
- Day 1: Order peptide batch
- Day 5: Peptide received
- Day 7: Verify batch CoA matches documentation

Weeks 4-15:
- Execute research protocol
- Document storage conditions weekly
- Record any adverse events
- Maintain training records

Week 16:
- Finalize data
- Decommission unused peptide (per institutional waste protocol)
- Report results to IBC (if required)

Weeks 17-20:
- Analyze data, write manuscript
- Disclose compliance details in Methods
- Submit for publication

Ongoing (5-7 years):
- Retain all documentation (regulatory requirement)
```

## Conclusion

**Key compliance points:**

1. **IBC approval** is required (check with your institution)
2. **Supplier documentation** must be complete (CoA, batch #, purity)
3. **Protocol documentation** must be thorough (methods, hazards, safety)
4. **Publication disclosure** must include supplier, batch, and approval details
5. **Record retention** is mandatory (5-7 years minimum)

**Compliance protects:**
- Your research validity (defensible in peer review)
- Your institution (regulatory compliance)
- Your reputation (integrity & credibility)
- Future funding (NIH & NSF require documented compliance)
