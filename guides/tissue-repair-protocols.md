# Tissue Repair Peptides: BPC-157 & TB-500 Research Protocols

## Executive Summary

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) and TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4) are peptides with documented effects on tissue repair, wound healing, and angiogenesis (blood vessel formation). This guide provides detailed research protocols for investigating their effects.

**Key findings:**
- Both peptides promote collagen synthesis and fibroblast migration
- BPC-157: Localized tissue repair (injection site)
- TB-500: Systemic tissue repair (circulating)
- NOT on WADA prohibited list (athlete-safe research)
- Minimal systemic side effects (research-friendly)

---

## 1. Mechanisms of Tissue Repair

### BPC-157: Mechanism of Action

**Discovery:** Isolated from gastric juice (Body Protection Compound, first discovered in the stomach).

**Primary mechanism:**
- **Fibroblast activation:** Stimulates fibroblasts to migrate to injury sites
- **Collagen synthesis:** Increases Type I and III collagen production
- **Growth factor upregulation:** Increases VEGF (Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor) and bFGF (basic Fibroblast Growth Factor)
- **Angiogenesis:** Promotes new blood vessel formation
- **Anti-inflammatory:** Reduces TNF-α and IL-6 (pro-inflammatory cytokines)
- **Nitric oxide (NO):** Increases endothelial NO synthesis (improves blood flow)

**Result:** Accelerated tissue remodeling and repair.

### TB-500: Mechanism of Action

**Discovery:** Identified in thymus gland as part of immune regulation. Upregulated during wound healing.

**Primary mechanism:**
- **Actin dynamics:** Regulates actin filaments (cell structure/migration)
- **Cell migration:** Facilitates fibroblast & keratinocyte movement to injury
- **Angiogenesis:** Promotes new blood vessel formation (via VEGF)
- **Anti-inflammatory:** Reduces systemic inflammation
- **Growth factor binding:** Sequesters growth factors, delivering them locally to repair sites
- **Systemic circulation:** Unlike BPC-157, TB-500 circulates, affecting distant sites

**Result:** Systemic tissue repair response.

### Key Differences

| Property | BPC-157 | TB-500 |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **Origin** | Gastric juice | Thymus gland |
| **Route Effect** | Localized (injection site) | Systemic (circulating) |
| **Half-life** | ~5-6 hours (localized) | ~2 hours (circulating) |
| **Molecular Weight** | 1,438 Da | 4,963 Da |
| **Amino Acids** | 15 | 43 |
| **Application** | Tendon, ligament, muscle repair | Systemic tissue repair |
| **Frequency** | 1-2x daily (localized) | 2-3x weekly (systemic) |

## 2. BPC-157: Detailed Research Protocol

### BPC-157 Profile

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Sequence** | Gly-Glu-Pro-Pro-Pro-Gly-Lys-Pro-Pro-Pro-Pro-Pro-Gly-Lys-Pro |
| **Molecular Weight** | 1,438.6 Da |
| **Purity Target** | ≥95% (Aura Labs: 98%) |
| **Half-Life** | 5-6 hours (localized tissue) |
| **Peak Concentration** | 1-2 hours post-injection |
| **Typical Research Dose** | 10-20 mcg per injection |
| **Frequency** | 1-2x daily |
| **Route** | Subcutaneous (localized), intraperitoneal (systemic), oral |
| **Storage** | -20°C or -80°C (lyophilized) |

### Protocol 1: Tendon Repair Model (Rat Tail Tendon)

**Objective:** Investigate BPC-157 effects on tendon healing post-injury.

**Timeline:** 8 weeks

**Group design:**

| Group | Treatment | Dose | Frequency | N |
|-------|-----------|------|-----------|---|
| **Control** | Vehicle (saline) | 0.5 mL | Daily | 10 |
| **Low Dose** | BPC-157 | 10 mcg | Daily | 10 |
| **High Dose** | BPC-157 | 20 mcg | Daily | 10 |

**Experimental design:**

```
Week 1: Baseline
- Randomize rats into 3 groups
- Obtain baseline tendon strength measurements (if non-invasive imaging available)

Week 1 (Day 3): Injury
- Surgical transection of rat tail tendon
- Immediate post-operative pain assessment
- Begin treatment (Day 1 post-injury)

Weeks 1-8: Treatment
- Daily subcutaneous injection at injury site (0.5 mL volume)
- Control = saline
- Treatment = BPC-157 (10 mcg or 20 mcg in 0.5 mL saline)
- Pain assessment (0-3 scale) at Days 1, 3, 7, 14, 21
- Mobility assessment (range of motion) at Weeks 1, 2, 4, 8

Week 8: Euthanasia & Analysis
- Harvest tendon tissue
- Tensile strength testing (will tendon break, at what force?)
- Histology (collagen deposition, fibroblast infiltration)
- Immunohistochemistry (VEGF, bFGF, collagen type I/III staining)
- Gene expression (RNA-seq for collagen, growth factor genes)
```

**Dosing protocol:**

```
Day 1 post-injury:
- Anesthetize rat (ketamine/xylazine)
- Inject 0.5 mL solution subcutaneously at injury site
- Mark injection site with temporary marking
- Assess pain (hindlimb withdrawal response)

Days 2-7: Same as Day 1
Days 8-56: Same frequency, monitor for signs of infection or adverse reaction
```

**Measurement parameters:**

| Parameter | Timing | Method |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Pain Severity** | Days 1, 3, 7, 14, 21 | Von Frey filament test (withdrawal threshold) |
| **Mobility/ROM** | Weeks 1, 2, 4, 8 | Video analysis of tail movement |
| **Wound Closure** | Weeks 1, 2, 4, 8 | Photographic documentation + measurement |
| **Tensile Strength** | Week 8 | Universal testing machine (force at break) |
| **Histology** | Week 8 | H&E staining, collagen density quantification |
| **Immunostaining** | Week 8 | VEGF, bFGF, collagen I/III expression |
| **Gene Expression** | Week 8 | qRT-PCR for repair-related genes |

**Expected outcomes:**
- BPC-157 groups show faster pain resolution (lower pain scores Week 2-4)
- BPC-157 groups show improved ROM (greater tail mobility Week 4-8)
- BPC-157 groups show higher tensile strength (20-40% improvement at Week 8)
- Histology shows increased collagen deposition in BPC-157 groups
- Gene expression shows upregulation of repair genes (COL1A1, VEGF, bFGF)

**Statistical analysis:**
- ANOVA (comparing 3 groups across timepoints)
- Post-hoc Tukey test (pairwise comparisons)
- Two-way ANOVA (group × time interaction)
- Significance: p < 0.05

### Protocol 2: Muscle Repair Model (Muscle Injury)

**Objective:** Investigate BPC-157 effects on skeletal muscle regeneration.

**Timeline:** 4 weeks (muscle heals faster than tendon)

**Injury model:**
- Surgical excision of 20% of gastrocnemius muscle
- Immediate BPC-157 treatment begins

**Treatment groups:**

| Group | Treatment | Dose | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Control** | Saline | 0.5 mL | Daily (local) |
| **BPC-157** | BPC-157 | 20 mcg | 2x daily (local) |

**Measurements:**

| Parameter | Timing | Method |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Muscle Strength** | Weeks 1, 2, 3, 4 | Grip strength test |
| **Muscle Mass** | Weeks 1, 2, 4 | Gastrocnemius wet weight (harvested) |
| **Regeneration** | Week 4 | Histology: new muscle fiber count |
| **Protein Synthesis** | Week 4 | Immunostaining: myosin heavy chain |
| **Gene Expression** | Week 4 | qRT-PCR: myogenic genes (MyoD, Myogenin) |

**Expected outcomes:**
- BPC-157 shows faster strength recovery (Week 2-3)
- BPC-157 shows greater muscle regeneration (higher myofiber count at Week 4)
- Gene expression shows upregulated myogenic factors

## 3. TB-500: Detailed Research Protocol

### TB-500 Profile

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Sequence** | Ac-SDKP peptide (N-terminal of thymosin beta-4) |
| **Full name** | Thymosin Beta-4 (Tβ4) or Ac-SDKP |
| **Molecular Weight** | 4,963 Da |
| **Purity Target** | ≥95% (Aura Labs: 98%) |
| **Half-Life** | ~2 hours (circulating) |
| **Peak Concentration** | 15-30 minutes post-injection |
| **Typical Research Dose** | 5-10 mg per injection |
| **Frequency** | 2-3x weekly (systemic dosing) |
| **Route** | Subcutaneous or intravenous |
| **Storage** | -20°C or -80°C (lyophilized) |

### Protocol 1: Systemic Tissue Repair (Multi-Site Injury)

**Objective:** Investigate TB-500 effects on systemic tissue repair after multiple injuries.

**Timeline:** 4 weeks

**Injury model:**
- Multiple small skin wounds (6-8 mm biopsy punch, dorsal surface, 4 sites per rat)
- Standardized muscle contusion (blunt force)
- Measured at multiple timepoints

**Treatment groups:**

| Group | Treatment | Dose | Frequency | N |
|-------|-----------|------|-----------|---|
| **Control** | Saline | 0.5 mL | 2x weekly | 12 |
| **TB-500** | TB-500 | 5 mg | 2x weekly (IV) | 12 |
| **TB-500 Hi** | TB-500 | 10 mg | 2x weekly (IV) | 12 |

**Experimental design:**

```
Week 1 (Days 1-3): Baseline
- Randomize rats into 3 groups
- Baseline measurements (weight, activity)

Week 1 (Day 4): Injury
- 4 skin punch biopsies (dorsal surface, 6 mm diameter)
- Muscle contusion (controlled blunt force to hindlimb)
- Begin treatment immediately post-injury

Weeks 1-4: Treatment
- Intravenous injection 2x weekly (Monday, Thursday)
- Skin wound monitoring: Photographic + measurement
- Mobility/activity: Cage activity monitoring
- Pain: Von Frey withdrawal test

Week 4: Euthanasia & Analysis
- Harvest skin wounds (histology)
- Harvest muscle contusion site
- Tensile strength testing (skin)
- Histology: collagen deposition, neovascularization
- Immunostaining: VEGF, CD31 (endothelial marker)
- Gene expression: angiogenic genes
```

**Dosing protocol (IV injection):**

```
TB-500 reconstitution:
- Lyophilized TB-500 (5 mg or 10 mg vial)
- Reconstitute in 0.5 mL sterile saline
- Concentration: 10 mg/mL (5 mg dose) or 20 mg/mL (10 mg dose)
- Draw up 0.5 mL in insulin syringe

IV injection technique:
- Tail vein injection (conscious rat, briefly restrained)
- Inject 0.5 mL bolus slowly over 10-15 seconds
- Observe for swelling (extravasation) for 5 minutes
- If extravasation occurs, note and prepare alternate tail vein for next injection

Schedule:
- Monday 10:00 AM, Thursday 10:00 AM (consistent timing)
- Continue for 4 weeks (8 total injections)
```

**Measurement parameters:**

| Parameter | Timing | Method |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Wound Closure** | Days 1, 3, 7, 14, 21, 28 | Photographic measurement |
| **Wound Area** | Days 1-28 | ImageJ analysis (% of original area closed) |
| **Pain Response** | Weeks 1, 2, 3, 4 | Von Frey filament test |
| **Mobility** | Weeks 1, 2, 3, 4 | Open field activity test (distance traveled) |
| **Wound Tensile** | Week 4 | Skin tensile strength testing |
| **Histology** | Week 4 | H&E collagen quantification |
| **Angiogenesis** | Week 4 | CD31 immunostaining (vessel density) |
| **Gene Expression** | Week 4 | qRT-PCR: VEGF, FGF, angiogenic genes |

**Expected outcomes:**
- TB-500 shows faster wound closure (% healed higher by Day 21)
- TB-500 shows increased neovascularization (CD31+ vessel density)
- TB-500 shows enhanced gene expression (VEGF, FGF upregulation)
- TB-500 shows improved mobility (higher activity Week 1-2, faster return to normal)

### Protocol 2: Cardiac Tissue Repair (Post-MI Model)

**Objective:** Investigate TB-500 effects on cardiac repair after myocardial infarction (MI).

**Note:** This is an advanced protocol suitable for specialized cardiac research labs.

**Timeline:** 4-12 weeks

**Injury model:**
- Surgical ligation of left anterior descending (LAD) coronary artery
- Creates controlled myocardial infarction
- Immediate TB-500 treatment begins

**Treatment:**

| Group | Treatment | Dose | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Control** | Saline | 0.5 mL | 2x weekly (IV) |
| **TB-500** | TB-500 | 5 mg | 2x weekly (IV) |

**Measurements:**

| Parameter | Timing | Method |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Cardiac Function** | Baseline, Days 3, 7, 21, 28 | Echocardiography (EF, FS, LV dimensions) |
| **Scar Size** | Weeks 4, 12 | MRI or histology |
| **Angiogenesis** | Week 12 | CD31 staining (vessel density in scar) |
| **Fibrosis** | Week 12 | Masson's trichrome staining |
| **Gene Expression** | Weeks 4, 12 | qRT-PCR: angiogenic, anti-fibrotic genes |

**Expected outcomes:**
- TB-500 shows better ejection fraction (EF) at Week 4 and beyond
- TB-500 shows smaller scar size
- TB-500 shows increased neovascularization in scar region
- TB-500 shows reduced myocardial fibrosis

## 4. Comparative BPC-157 vs. TB-500 Studies

**If comparing both peptides:**

### Design

```
Group 1: Control (saline)
Group 2: BPC-157 (20 mcg 2x daily, local injection)
Group 3: TB-500 (5 mg 2x weekly, IV)
Group 4: BPC-157 + TB-500 (combination)
```

### Measurements (shared across groups)

| Parameter | BPC-157 advantage | TB-500 advantage |
|-----------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Localized repair** | Superior (direct injection) | Inferior (systemic) |
| **Systemic effects** | Limited (local) | Superior (circulating) |
| **Multi-site healing** | Poor | Good |
| **Single site healing** | Excellent | Moderate |

### Expected outcomes
- BPC-157 superior for localized tendon/muscle repair
- TB-500 superior for systemic/multi-site tissue repair
- Combination may provide synergistic effects

## 5. Sample Size & Statistical Power

### Power Analysis Example

**Hypothesis:** BPC-157 increases tendon tensile strength by 30% vs. control.

**Assumptions:**
- Control group: Mean 2.0 ± 0.4 kg (standard deviation)
- BPC-157 group: Mean 2.6 ± 0.4 kg (30% improvement)
- Effect size: 0.6 (medium effect)
- Power: 0.80 (detect true difference 80% of the time)
- α (significance): 0.05

**Sample size needed (per group): 18 rats**

**Total sample: 3 groups × 18 = 54 rats**

### Calculation
Using G*Power software or online calculators:
- Two-sample t-test
- Effect size (Cohen's d): 0.6
- Power: 0.80
- α: 0.05
- Result: n = 18 per group

## 6. Reconstitution & Administration

### BPC-157 Preparation

```
Lyophilized powder to liquid reconstitution:

Starting material:
- 1 vial BPC-157 powder (e.g., 10 mg)
- Sterile saline (0.9% NaCl) or water for injection

Concentration choice (depends on dose):
- For 10 mcg dose: Prepare 10 mcg/100 µL (0.1 mg/mL)
- For 20 mcg dose: Prepare 20 mcg/100 µL (0.2 mg/mL)

Preparation:
1. Aseptically add saline to vial (e.g., 10 mL for 1 mg/mL stock)
2. Gently swirl (do not shake vigorously - causes foaming)
3. Allow 5 minutes for complete dissolution
4. Verify clarity (clear, colorless to pale yellow solution)
5. Draw up required volume in insulin syringe (100 µL for 10 mcg)

Storage:
- Reconstituted at 4°C: 2-4 weeks
- Reconstituted at -20°C: 3-6 months
- Freeze in small aliquots to avoid repeated thaws

Administration:
- Subcutaneous injection: Small fold of skin on back
- Inject slowly over 10-15 seconds
- Rotate injection sites (to avoid lipohypertrophy)
```

### TB-500 Preparation

```
Similar to BPC-157 with higher molecular weight:

Starting material:
- 1 vial TB-500 powder (e.g., 5 mg or 10 mg)
- Sterile saline or bacteriostatic saline (recommended for multi-use vials)

Concentration:
- For 5 mg dose: Prepare 5 mg/mL stock
- For 10 mg dose: Prepare 10 mg/mL stock

Preparation:
1. Add appropriate volume of saline to vial
2. Gently roll vial between palms (gentler than swirling)
3. Allow 10-15 minutes for complete dissolution (slower than BPC-157)
4. Verify clarity
5. Draw up required volume

For IV administration:
- Further dilute in saline if needed (e.g., 5 mg in 5 mL saline = 1 mg/mL)
- Use 25G needle for tail vein injection
- Inject slowly to avoid hemolysis

Storage:
- Reconstituted at 4°C: 2-4 weeks (if using bacteriostatic saline)
- Freeze at -20°C: 3-6 months
```

## 7. Monitoring & Safety

### Adverse Events to Monitor

| Event | Sign | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| **Injection site reaction** | Redness, swelling, nodule | Alternate injection site, document |
| **Infection** | Pus, heat, animal lethargy | Euthanize, culture site |
| **Allergic reaction** | Rash, swelling, respiratory distress | Stop treatment, provide care, monitor |
| **Systemic toxicity** | Weight loss, lethargy, appetite loss | Reduce dose or discontinue, monitor |

### Animal welfare monitoring

- **Daily:** Observe injection sites (redness, infection)
- **Twice weekly:** Assess mobility and pain (Von Frey test)
- **Weekly:** Weigh animals (ensure normal growth)
- **As needed:** Euthanize animals showing severe adverse effects

## 9. Expected Timelines & Results

### BPC-157 Expected Timeline

| Timepoint | Observation |
|-----------|------------|
| **Hour 1-2** | Peak local concentration, initial fibroblast activation |
| **Days 1-3** | Pain reduction (if analgesics not used, BPC-157 reduces pain) |

| **Days 1-3** | Systemic effects begin (reduced inflammation) |
| **Days 7-14** | Wound closure visible, neovascularization begins |

| **Weeks 3-4** | Tissue repair progressing across multiple sites |
| **Weeks 4-12** | Long-term remodeling (especially in cardiac models) |

## Conclusion

**BPC-157:**
- Best for localized, acute tissue repair
- Inject directly at injury site
- Daily dosing (1-2x)
- Peak effects: Days 7-21

**TB-500:**
- Best for systemic/multi-site tissue repair
- Systemic administration (IV or frequent SC)
- Less frequent dosing (2-3x weekly)
- Peak effects: Weeks 2-4

**When to use each:**
- Single tendon injury → BPC-157
- Multiple injuries + systemic healing → TB-500
- Cardiac repair → TB-500
- Muscle/tendon → BPC-157 or both
