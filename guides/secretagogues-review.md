# Growth Hormone Secretagogues: A Comprehensive Research Review

## Executive Summary

Growth hormone secretagogues (GHS) are peptides and small molecules that stimulate the pituitary gland to release human growth hormone (GH). This guide reviews the major classes, mechanisms, dosing, and research applications for peptide researchers.

**Key compounds covered:**
- Ipamorelin
- CJC-1295 (with DAC and without)
- GHRP-6 / GHRP-2
- MK-677 (oral alternative)
- Hexarelin

---

## 1. Mechanism of Growth Hormone Secretion

### Normal GH Regulation

Growth hormone is released by somatotroph cells in the anterior pituitary in response to:

1. **GHRH (Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone)** — stimulates release
2. **Somatostatin (SST)** — inhibits release
3. **Ghrelin** — natural GHS (appetite hormone, also stimulates GH)

The pituitary maintains a dynamic balance between stimulation and inhibition, releasing ~1-2 ng/mL of GH in pulses (especially during sleep and exercise).

### How Secretagogues Work

**GHS bind to ghrelin receptors (GHS-R1a)** on somatotroph cells, triggering calcium release and GH secretion independent of GHRH (though often synergistic).

**Three classes of mechanisms:**

#### Class 1: GHRP Peptides (GH-Releasing Peptides)
- Ipamorelin, GHRP-6, GHRP-2, Hexarelin
- Bind GHS-R1a directly
- No significant side effects (unlike GHRPs)
- Peak GH at 30-60 minutes post-injection

#### Class 2: GHRH Analogs
- CJC-1295 (modified GHRH)
- Extends GHRH half-life via DAC modification
- Longer duration (days to weeks)
- Can cause GH elevation for extended periods

#### Class 3: Oral Mimetics
- MK-677 (ibutamoren)
- Ghrelin receptor agonist (oral)
- Convenient, but less potent than injected peptides

## 2. Ipamorelin — The Selective Ghrelin Agonist

### Profile

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Class** | GHRP peptide |
| **Sequence** | Ac-Aib-His-D2Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2 |
| **Molecular Weight** | 711.87 Da |
| **Purity Target** | ≥95% (Aura Labs: 98%) |
| **Synthesis** | Patented by Novo Nordisk (now off-patent) |
| **Half-Life** | ~2 hours |
| **Peak GH** | 30-60 minutes post-injection |
| **Typical Dose (Research)** | 100-200 mcg per injection |
| **Frequency** | 1-3x daily |
| **Route** | Subcutaneous or intramuscular injection |

### Mechanism

Ipamorelin is a **selective agonist** of ghrelin receptors with minimal off-target activity.

**Why "selective"?**
- Binds GHS-R1a (primary target) with high affinity
- Does NOT significantly bind other GH-related receptors
- Does NOT suppress somatostatin
- Does NOT elevate prolactin, ACTH, or cortisol

This selectivity makes ipamorelin ideal for research where you want GH elevation without confounding hormonal changes.

### Research Applications

**Tissue repair & regeneration**
- GH promotes myofibril growth and collagen synthesis
- Studied in bone healing, tendon repair, muscle recovery

**Metabolic studies**
- GH increases lipolysis (fat burning)
- Increases insulin-like growth factor-1 (IGF-1)
- Studied in metabolic dysfunction research

**Anti-aging research**
- GH decline with age (somatopause)
- Ipamorelin used to study age-related GH deficiency

**Sleep & recovery**
- GH naturally peaks during sleep
- Ipamorelin timing studies for sleep optimization

### Comparative Advantages

vs. GHRP-6:
- No prolactin elevation (GHRP-6 stimulates prolactin significantly)
- Less appetite stimulation (important for controlled studies)

vs. CJC-1295:
- Shorter duration (better for acute experiments)
- Easier to control GH timing

vs. Hexarelin:
- Better safety profile (no cortisol elevation)
- More selective GH release

### Dosing for Research

| Application | Dose | Frequency | Duration |
|-------------|------|-----------|----------|
| **Acute GH study** | 100-200 mcg | Single injection | Monitor 2-3 hours |
| **Daily protocol** | 100 mcg | 1-2x daily | 4-12 weeks |
| **Chronic study** | 200 mcg | 2-3x daily | 12+ weeks |
| **Pharmacokinetic** | 50-500 mcg | Single | Vary for curve |

### Storage & Stability

- **Form:** Lyophilized powder (most stable)
- **Storage:** -20°C or -80°C (protects from degradation)
- **Shelf life:** 2-3 years at -20°C, 5+ years at -80°C
- **Reconstitution:** Sterile saline or water for injection
- **Reconstituted shelf life:** 2-4 weeks at 4°C (use fresh when possible)

## 3. CJC-1295 — Extended-Release GHRH Analog

### Profile

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Full Name** | Tetrasubstituted GH-Releasing Hormone (GHRH) analog |
| **Sequence** | GH-RH(1-29)-albumen binding domain |
| **Molecular Weight** | 3,372 Da (with DAC) |
| **Purity Target** | ≥95% (Aura Labs: 98%) |
| **Half-Life (with DAC)** | 6-8 days |
| **Half-Life (without DAC)** | 30-40 minutes |
| **Peak GH** | 4-6 hours (without DAC); days (with DAC) |
| **Typical Dose** | 100-300 mcg per injection |
| **Frequency (with DAC)** | 1-2x weekly |
| **Frequency (without DAC)** | 1-3x daily |
| **Route** | Subcutaneous injection |

### Two Versions: DAC vs. Non-DAC

#### CJC-1295 WITH DAC (Drug Affinity Complex)

The "DAC" modification attaches the peptide to albumin-binding domain, extending circulation time dramatically.

**Advantages:**
- Longer half-life (6-8 days vs. 30 minutes)
- Steady GH elevation over days
- Less frequent dosing (1-2x weekly)
- Ideal for chronic elevation studies

**Disadvantages:**
- Longer time to reach steady state (3-4 weeks)
- Cannot quickly adjust dose if unwanted effects
- GH elevation is plateau, not pulsatile

**Research use:**
- Chronic GH deficiency studies
- Long-term metabolic changes
- 8-12+ week protocols

#### CJC-1295 WITHOUT DAC

The unmodified GHRH analog without albumin-binding.

**Advantages:**
- Rapid peak (30-45 minutes)
- Shorter half-life (easier to adjust)
- More natural pulsatile pattern
- Better for acute studies

**Disadvantages:**
- Frequent dosing required (1-3x daily)
- Less convenient
- Higher cost if dosing frequently

**Research use:**
- Acute GH secretion studies
- Pharmacokinetic curves
- Short-term protocols (4-8 weeks)

### Mechanism

CJC-1295 is a modified version of natural GHRH. It binds GHRH receptors on somatotroph cells and triggers cAMP signaling, leading to GH release.

**Key difference from ipamorelin:**
- Ipamorelin: Binds ghrelin receptors (GHS-R1a)
- CJC-1295: Binds GHRH receptors (direct GHRH analog)

These are **synergistic** — often combined in research protocols.

## 4. GHRP-6 & GHRP-2 — Older Secretagogues

### GHRP-6

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Sequence** | His-D-Trp-Ala-Trp-D-Phe-Lys-NH2 |
| **MW** | 873.02 Da |
| **Half-Life** | 30-40 minutes |
| **Peak GH** | 30-60 minutes |
| **Side Effects** | Prolactin ↑, cortisol ↑, appetite ↑ |

**Research relevance:** Older standard, largely replaced by ipamorelin due to side effects. Still used in specific studies where these effects are acceptable or relevant.

| **Side Effects** | Prolactin ↑, cortisol ↑↑ (more than GHRP-6) |

**Research relevance:** More potent GH release than GHRP-6, but cortisol elevation is problematic. Used in specific research where cortisol elevation is being studied.

## 5. Hexarelin — Ultra-Potent GHRP

### Profile

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Sequence** | His-D-2Nal-Ala-Trp-D-Phe-Lys-NH2 |
| **Molecular Weight** | 897.05 Da |
| **Half-Life** | 30-40 minutes |
| **Peak GH** | 15-30 minutes |
| **GH Release** | 6-7x higher than GHRP-6 |
| **Prolactin** | Moderate elevation |
| **Cortisol** | Moderate elevation |

**Advantages:** Potent GH release in small doses.

**Disadvantages:** High potency → risk of GH receptor desensitization with chronic use.

**Research use:** Limited. Used primarily in acute GH release studies or when maximal GH elevation is critical.

## 6. MK-677 (Ibutamoren) — Oral Alternative

### Profile

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Class** | Small molecule (non-peptide) |
| **Mechanism** | Ghrelin receptor agonist (oral) |
| **Bioavailability** | ~70% oral |
| **Half-Life** | 4-6 hours |
| **Peak GH** | 1-2 hours post-dose |
| **Typical Dose** | 10-25 mg daily |
| **Route** | Oral (tablet/capsule) |

### Advantages

- **Oral:** No injections required
- **Convenient:** Can dose multiple times daily
- **Cost-effective:** Lower per-dose cost
- **Compliance:** Easier for long-term studies

### Disadvantages

- **Lower potency:** Less GH elevation than ipamorelin/CJC
- **Appetite stimulation:** More significant than peptide secretagogues
- **Cortisol:** Moderate elevation (more than ipamorelin)
- **Prolactin:** Mild elevation

### Research Applications

- Convenient for chronic studies (12+ weeks)
- Oral bioavailability research
- Patient tolerance studies
- Metabolic studies where injection compliance is a barrier

## 7. Synergistic Combinations

Researchers often combine secretagogues for enhanced effects.

### ipamorelin + CJC-1295 (Popular Combination)

**Why combine?**
- Ipamorelin binds **ghrelin receptors**
- CJC-1295 binds **GHRH receptors**
- Both pathways activate somatotroph cells synergistically
- Combined GH elevation 30-50% higher than either alone

**Typical protocol:**
- CJC-1295 (with DAC): 100 mcg 2x weekly (baseline elevation)
- Ipamorelin: 100 mcg 3x daily (acute boosts)
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Expected GH elevation: 50-100% above baseline

### Research use:
- Chronic metabolic studies
- Muscle hypertrophy protocols
- Tissue repair acceleration

## 8. Dosing & Pharmacokinetic Considerations

### Calculating Doses

**Typical conversions:**

| Peptide | Research Dose | Per kg body weight | Per m² body surface |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Ipamorelin** | 100-200 mcg | 1-3 mcg/kg | 50-100 mcg/m² |
| **CJC-1295** | 100-300 mcg | 1-4 mcg/kg | 50-150 mcg/m² |
| **GHRP-6** | 50-100 mcg | 0.5-1.5 mcg/kg | 25-50 mcg/m² |
| **MK-677** | 10-25 mg | 0.1-0.3 mg/kg | 5-15 mg/m² |

### Injection Timing

**Fasted vs. Fed:**
- GH release is **inhibited by glucose/insulin**
- Optimal: Inject during fasted state (morning, before meals)
- If studying fed response, document meal timing relative to injection

**Sleep & GH:**
- GH naturally peaks during sleep (slow-wave sleep, NREM Stage 3)
- Evening/bedtime dosing may enhance natural pulsatile pattern
- Morning dosing useful for acute study protocols

### Tolerance & Desensitization

**Continuous vs. pulsatile dosing:**
- Continuous elevation (e.g., CJC-1295 with DAC) can lead to receptor desensitization over 8-12 weeks
- Pulsatile dosing (e.g., ipamorelin 3x daily) may maintain sensitivity longer
- Cycling protocols: 8 weeks on, 4 weeks off (prevents desensitization)

## 9. Safety Considerations & Monitoring

### Potential Effects

| Effect | Ipamorelin | GHRP-6 | CJC-1295 | Risk Level |
|--------|-----------|--------|----------|-----------|
| **GH elevation** | Yes | Yes | Yes | Moderate |
| **IGF-1 elevation** | Yes | Yes | Yes | Moderate |
| **Prolactin elevation** | Minimal | Moderate | Minimal | Low-Moderate |
| **Cortisol elevation** | Minimal | Moderate | Minimal | Low-Moderate |
| **Glucose changes** | Minimal | Minimal | Minimal | Low |
| **Appetite increase** | Minimal | High | Minimal | Low-Moderate |

### Monitoring Recommendations

**Baseline testing (before protocol):**
- IGF-1 level
- Prolactin level
- Cortisol level (AM, fasting)
- Glucose & insulin (fasting)

**During protocol (every 4 weeks):**
- IGF-1 (tracks GH elevation)
- Prolactin (if using GHRPs; ipamorelin/CJC minimal effect)

**End of protocol:**
- Full panel (same as baseline)
- Tolerance/desensitization assessment

## 11. Regulatory & Compliance Notes

### Research-Grade vs. Pharmaceutical-Grade

**This guide assumes research-grade compounds:**
- ≥95% purity (Aura Labs: 98%)
- For in vitro and animal studies
- NOT for human use (except in approved clinical trials)

Suppliers who claim "pharmaceutical-grade" for research application may be misrepresenting. Verify GMP certification if claiming pharmaceutical-grade.

### Publication Compliance

- **Disclose supplier** (Aura Labs, with batch #)
- **Report purity** (%)
- **State intended use** ("Research use only")
- **Include CoA reference** ("Available upon request")

4. Plan monitoring protocol (IGF-1, prolactin, cortisol)
5. Document batch details for reproducibility
